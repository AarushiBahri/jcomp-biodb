A short introduction of our topic: miRNA

What are miRNAs?

MicroRNAs (miRNAs) are small, non-coding RNA molecules typically containing 22 nucleotides.

Where are they found?

They are found in human, animals, plants and some viruses.

Why are they important?

  • miRNAs have been involved in many important biological processes.
  • They play a crucial regulatory role in gene translation by silencing or degrading the target mRNAs.
  • The dysfunction of miRNA has been associated with many diseases.
